Heritage Lotus Root and Pork Rib Soup
Perfect for: Overcast, rainy days or chilly, descending temperatures.

Benefits: Warms the stomach and dispels internal "dampness" or cold.

The Methodology:
Preparation: Peel the lotus root and cut into hearty, oblique chunks.

Blanching: Place the pork ribs in cold water and bring to a boil; this removes impurities, ensuring a crystal-clear broth.

Simmering: In a heavy-bottomed pot or clay vessel, combine the ribs, lotus root, and thick slices of fresh ginger.

The Wait: Allow the soup to simmer on a gentle, low flame for at least 2 hours. This slow extraction allows the lotus root to become tender and the marrow to enrich the broth.

Finishing: Only add a pinch of fine sea salt just before serving to preserve the natural, delicate sweetness of the ingredients.
